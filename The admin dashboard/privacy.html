Privacy Policy

How BullPort may collect, use, share, retain and protect personal data in the Client Portal and related account workflows.

Privacy summary: This policy explains how BullPort may collect, use, share, retain and protect personal information in the Client Portal. The current portal may operate as a demo/static environment, but this policy also describes data categories that may apply when live onboarding and account workflows are enabled.

Last updated: July 17, 2026

1. Who we are

BullPort provides a client portal for broker-style account, onboarding, portfolio, wallet, market, report and support workflows. For privacy questions, contact privacy@bullport.com or write to BullPort, London, United Kingdom.

2. Personal data we may process

Depending on the feature used, BullPort may process identity data, contact data, KYC and verification documents, financial profile information, trading and investment activity, wallet and funding records, dividend or profit records, communications, device and usage data, cookie data, marketing preferences, regulatory records and audit logs.

3. Why we use personal data

We use personal data to create and secure accounts, operate the portal, support users, process onboarding, conduct KYC/AML and sanctions checks, assess suitability or eligibility, manage wallet and investment workflows, produce reports, handle complaints, prevent fraud, improve the service and comply with legal obligations.

4. Lawful basis

Depending on the activity and jurisdiction, BullPort may rely on contract necessity, legal obligations, legitimate interests, consent, or another lawful basis recognised by applicable data protection law.

Where consent is required, such as certain marketing or optional analytics cookies, you may withdraw consent where the law allows.

5. Current demo note

The current portal may be configured so that some forms only store information locally in the browser, submit to a test endpoint, or do not submit to a live backend. Submitting information in the current demo does not by itself open a live trading account or complete live regulatory onboarding.

6. Cookies and similar technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to keep the Client Portal secure, remember preferences, understand usage patterns and, where you consent, support analytics or marketing. Strictly necessary cookies are used for basic site functions such as session continuity, security and consent storage. Optional cookies are used only where required consent has been collected.

Read the Cookie Policy for more detail.

7. Marketing communications

BullPort may send service messages about your account or portal activity. Marketing messages are sent where permitted by law or where you have consented. You can opt out of marketing communications, although we may still send important service, legal or security messages.

8. Sharing personal data

We may share personal data with hosting providers, security providers, identity and KYC providers, payment or reconciliation providers, market-data or reporting tools, professional advisers, regulators, law enforcement, group companies and other service providers where reasonably required for the purposes described in this policy.

9. International transfers

Personal data may be processed in countries outside your country of residence. Where required, BullPort will use appropriate safeguards or another lawful transfer mechanism.

10. Data security

BullPort uses reasonable technical and organisational measures designed to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, misuse, alteration or disclosure. No internet service is completely risk-free, so users should keep credentials secure and report suspected compromise promptly.

11. Retention

We keep personal data only for as long as needed for the relevant purpose, including account operation, legal compliance, audit, tax, dispute handling, fraud prevention and regulatory record keeping. Demo or test data should be kept for shorter periods where practical.

12. Your rights

Depending on applicable law, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, object to processing, transfer data, withdraw consent, and avoid certain automated decisions. To exercise rights, contact privacy@bullport.com.

13. Automated decision-making

BullPort may use rules or scoring to support security, KYC, risk or eligibility workflows. Where legally required, users may request human review of decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects.

14. Children

The Client Portal is not intended for children or minors unless a lawful custodial or guardian arrangement is expressly supported.

15. Breaches

If a personal data breach occurs, BullPort will assess it and make notifications to affected individuals or authorities where required by applicable law.

16. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in the portal, law, technology or business operations. The last-updated date will show when the latest version became effective.
